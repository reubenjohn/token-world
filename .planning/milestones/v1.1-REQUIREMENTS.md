## Milestone vision

v1.0 proved the simulation engine could yield, spawn an operator authoring pass,
and resume once a new mechanic landed. What it could not prove was that the
loop held up *unattended* — without a human clicking through prompts. v1.1
took the mandate *"make it feel possible — maybe even inevitable — that
interesting universes can emerge from play without hand-holding"* and delivered
three surfaces for that outcome: the Operator CLI (agents query a running
universe), the NiceGUI Dashboard (humans watch it move), and the Emergence
Substrate (subagents close the author-resume loop over the user's Claude
subscription at $0 marginal cost). An unattended Willowbrook overnight run
authored 11 novel mechanics end-to-end. The v1.0 Known Gaps were also closed
out during the warm-up.

## Known Gaps at v1.1 close (carried into v1.2)

These items were identified in session 4 but not closed before the v1.2 scoping pass.
They are preserved in `.planning/REQUIREMENTS.md` as REQ-V12-EMERGE-01 and
REQ-V12-EMERGE-02 respectively.

- **REQ-EMERGE-05 — mechanic overlap detector:** before authoring, operator should diff a proposed verb+watches against existing mechanics; prefer edit-existing when overlap exceeds threshold. Emergent universes accumulate near-duplicates without this.
- **REQ-EMERGE-07 — operator decision log richness:** `<universe>/operator-log.jsonl` exists but only contains yield/resolve events. Phase 12 was supposed to enrich with the subagent's full reasoning JSON; not landed before v1.1 close.

These were the only items not shipped at milestone close; every other item in
the v1.1 scope delivered.
